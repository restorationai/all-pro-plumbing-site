One phone number for the pipe under your slab and the condenser unit on your roof. That's the idea behind All Pro Plumbing Heating and Air — a Bakersfield-based shop that handles the mechanical systems a house depends on without handing you off to a second contractor when the problem crosses a trade line.

## What we do

On any given morning the dispatch board might show a no-cool call in Oildale with temperatures already climbing past 95°F, a water heater that stopped producing hot water sometime Saturday night, a sewer line backing up into a shower pan, and a scheduled furnace installation waiting on a permit inspection. That mix — urgent and planned, plumbing and HVAC — is a normal day. Because we run both trades under one roof, the same team that replaces your water heater can also check the gas line feeding it, and the technician diagnosing your AC can flag the condensate drain that's quietly dripping toward your drywall. We're available around the clock, every day of the year, so a burst pipe at 2 a.m. or a dead heater on a holiday weekend gets a real response, not a voicemail.

## How we work

Before any work starts, we diagnose the problem and quote the repair. The number you approve is the number on the invoice. On installation and replacement jobs — water heaters, furnaces, heat pumps, AC systems — we pull the required permits and schedule the inspections those permits require. That step protects your home's resale record and keeps manufacturer warranties intact; skipping it saves an afternoon and creates problems that surface years later. Bakersfield's hard water accelerates scale buildup inside tank water heaters and heat exchangers, and the valley's summer heat loads push condensing units harder than manufacturers' specs assume in milder climates — we account for both when sizing and recommending equipment, not just when something fails.

When a plumbing repair uncovers water damage that's spread beyond the pipe itself — soaked subfloor, wet drywall, compromised insulation — that work falls outside our scope. Our sister company, [ProRestoration Services](https://prorestorationca.com), handles the drying, remediation, and rebuild side if you need a referral.

## Where we work

Our primary service area is Bakersfield and the surrounding communities throughout Kern County — including Oildale, Shafter, Wasco, Delano, Tehachapi, and the unincorporated areas between them. The Central Valley's climate and construction patterns shape a lot of what we see: slab-on-grade foundations that hide supply lines until a leak shows up as a warm spot on the floor, aging housing stock with galvanized pipe that's reached the end of its service life, and summers that run long enough to put real wear on HVAC equipment year after year. If you're outside these areas and unsure whether we cover your address, a quick call to (661) 863-9242 will get you a straight answer.
