## Why Taft Properties See Air Quality Issues

The petroleum industry that built Taft also shaped its environment in ways that show up inside homes. Particulate matter from active oil operations, combined with the fine silica dust common to the Kern County desert floor, finds its way through gaps in older construction that was never built to modern air-sealing standards. The workers' cottages spread through South Taft and Ford City often have original single-pane windows, uninsulated crawl spaces, and attic bypasses that let outdoor air — and everything in it — migrate freely into living spaces.

Compounding the problem is the region's extreme dryness. Relative humidity in Taft regularly drops below 20 percent in summer, which desiccates nasal passages, aggravates respiratory conditions, and causes wood framing and cabinetry to crack. At the same time, the same homes that dry out in summer can trap cooking odors, combustion byproducts from older gas appliances, and volatile organic compounds from aging building materials — because there is no controlled fresh-air exchange to dilute them.

## Our Indoor Air Quality Services Process in Taft

Every project starts with a diagnostic conversation about what you're noticing: persistent dust on surfaces near vents, dry throats every morning, musty odors when the HVAC kicks on, or allergy symptoms that improve when you leave the house. From there, the approach is matched to the home.

For older homes with existing forced-air systems — common in the neighborhoods around Taft College and Franklin Field — an HVAC filtration upgrade is often the fastest improvement. Swapping a standard 1-inch filter slot for a properly sized media filter or an electronically enhanced filter can remove particles the original equipment was never designed to catch. Where duct systems are intact and accessible, a UV air purifier installed at the air handler neutralizes airborne pathogens and mold spores before they recirculate.

For homes with severe dryness complaints, a whole home humidifier installation tied to the existing furnace or air handler adds controlled moisture without the maintenance burden of portable units. And when the source of an odor or health complaint isn't obvious, indoor air quality testing gives a factual baseline — measuring particulate levels, VOC concentrations, and humidity — before any equipment is recommended.

## Equipment and Methods We Use for Air Quality

The equipment selected for a Taft home has to hold up against conditions that would be unusual in a coastal California city. UV air purifiers for HVAC systems are rated by lamp intensity and chamber design — in a climate where the system runs hard for six or more months of the year, lamp longevity and ballast quality matter more than they would in a mild coastal market. Whole house air purifiers with activated carbon stages are particularly useful here because they address both particulates and the hydrocarbon-adjacent odors that can drift into homes near active oil infrastructure.

Humidifiers are sized to the home's square footage and the measured dryness of the local climate — a bypass humidifier that works adequately in Bakersfield proper may need to be stepped up to a fan-powered unit in Taft, where the air is drier and the heating season longer. All equipment is installed to manufacturer specifications and integrated with the existing thermostat or smart control where possible.
