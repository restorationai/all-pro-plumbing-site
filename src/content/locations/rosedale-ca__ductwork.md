## Why Rosedale Homes See Duct Problems Earlier Than You'd Expect

Most of Rosedale's housing stock was built between the mid-1990s and the 2020s — newer than much of Bakersfield proper, but that doesn't mean the ductwork is trouble-free. Kern County's extreme summer heat is the primary culprit. Attic temperatures in single-story homes along the Rosedale Highway retail corridor and into Rosedale Ranch regularly exceed 150°F from June through September. Flexible duct exposed to those temperatures degrades faster than manufacturer ratings assume: the outer jacket becomes brittle, inner liner separates from fittings, and the foil vapor barrier delaminates. The result is conditioned air bleeding directly into the attic before it ever reaches a bedroom.

Compounding the heat problem is the region's soil movement. Kern County's expansive clay soils shift seasonally, and in slab-on-grade homes — the dominant construction type throughout the 93312 and 93314 ZIP codes — that movement occasionally torques platform framing enough to pull duct connections loose at trunk-line joints. A connection that looked tight at rough-in can open a half-inch gap within a few heating seasons.

Finally, the area's hard water affects more than pipes. Evaporative coolers, which some older Rosedale properties still run alongside central AC, push mineral-laden air through duct systems and deposit scale inside metal trunk lines. That buildup narrows airflow and can accelerate corrosion at seams.

## Our Ductwork Repair and Installation Process in Rosedale

Every job starts with a leaky duct testing phase before any repair or replacement work begins. We use a duct blaster pressurization test to measure total duct leakage as a percentage of system airflow — this gives you a baseline number, not a guess. In Rosedale's newer homes, we frequently find leakage rates of 20–30% on systems that are only five to ten years old, almost always traced to failed mastic at flex-duct collars or disconnected joints in attic runs.

Once we have the test data, the repair scope becomes concrete:

- **Duct sealing**: We apply aerosol or brush-on mastic sealant at every joint and collar, then wrap with UL 181-rated foil tape. We do not use standard cloth duct tape — it fails within two to three years in high-attic-temperature environments like Rosedale's.
- **Flex duct replacement**: Sections with cracked liners or collapsed inner cores get swapped out for new insulated flex rated for the attic temperature range. We size replacement sections to match the original Manual D design, or correct undersizing if the original layout was the problem.
- **Full ductwork installation**: On new construction or whole-system replacements, we design the layout to current ACCA Manual D standards, pull the required Kern County mechanical permit, and schedule the rough-in inspection before drywall closes.

After repairs, we retest with the duct blaster to confirm leakage is within California Title 24 thresholds — typically under 6% for new installations and under 15% for repaired existing systems.
