## Our Sewer Line Repair and Replacement Process in Taft

Every job starts with a sewer camera inspection. We run a waterproof camera through the lateral from the cleanout — or from a pulled toilet if there's no accessible cleanout — and record exactly what's happening: root intrusion, offset joints, a collapsed section, heavy grease buildup, or a belly in the line where soil has shifted. You see the footage, and we talk through what it means before any repair is quoted.

From there, the right method depends on what the camera shows. Trenchless sewer repair — pipe lining or pipe bursting — works well when the pipe has a consistent path and the damage is distributed rather than concentrated. Pipe lining bonds a new epoxy sleeve to the inside of the existing pipe without excavation, which matters on Taft properties where driveways and landscaping sit directly over the lateral. Pipe bursting replaces the old pipe entirely by pulling a new HDPE line through the old one, fracturing it outward. When a section has fully collapsed or the pipe has gone badly out of grade, open-cut replacement is the honest answer — we'll tell you that upfront rather than sell you a liner that won't hold.

All work is permitted through Kern County and inspected before backfill. That inspection record protects you when you sell the property.
