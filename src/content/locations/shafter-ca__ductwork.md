## Our Ductwork Repair and Installation Process in Shafter

Every job starts with a leaky duct testing assessment before any cutting or patching begins. We use a duct blaster — a calibrated fan that pressurizes the duct system — to measure exactly how much air is escaping and where. That number gives you a baseline and tells us whether targeted duct sealing will solve the problem or whether a full duct replacement makes more economic sense.

For repair work, we use UL 181-rated mastic sealant and metal-backed tape at every joint — not the silver cloth tape that dries out and peels within a few years in Shafter's heat. For full ductwork installation or replacement, we size each run to match the actual load of the space it serves, accounting for ceiling height, window exposure, and the orientation of the home on the lot. A west-facing great room in a Gossamer Grove home needs more CFM than a shaded north bedroom, and a duct system that ignores that will leave one room roasting while another freezes.

After any significant repair or new installation, we re-test with the duct blaster to confirm the leakage rate meets California Title 24 standards before we close up the attic access.
