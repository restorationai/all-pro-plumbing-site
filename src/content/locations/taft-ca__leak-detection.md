## Our Leak Detection Process in Taft

Electronic leak detection lets us locate a problem without opening walls or jackhammering slabs until we know exactly where to dig. The process typically runs in three stages:

**Pressure isolation** — We shut off individual zones and monitor pressure decay with calibrated gauges. A line that loses pressure when isolated is the confirmed suspect; one that holds is ruled out. This narrows a whole-house mystery down to a single branch.

**Acoustic listening** — Sensitive ground microphones and pipe-contact sensors pick up the frequency signature of water escaping under pressure. In Taft's quieter residential streets — away from the highway traffic on CA-33 — ambient noise is low enough that acoustic equipment performs well even on buried lines.

**Thermal and tracer-gas confirmation** — For slab leaks, an infrared camera reads temperature differentials across the concrete surface. Where acoustic results are ambiguous, we introduce an inert tracer gas into the line and use a surface probe to pinpoint the exit point to within a few inches before any concrete is touched.

Once we've marked the location, we give you a written scope and cost before any repair work begins.
