## Why Taft Properties See More AC Repair Issues

The housing stock in Ford City and South Taft tells the story. Most of the workers' cottages built during the oil boom of the 1920s through 1950s were designed around wall furnaces and swamp coolers — central air conditioning was retrofitted decades later, often with equipment that was undersized for the square footage or installed without proper duct sealing. Those older duct systems leak conditioned air into attic spaces that regularly hit 140°F in summer, forcing the compressor to run almost continuously. Continuous runtime accelerates refrigerant loss through micro-cracks in copper line sets, wears down capacitors and contactors faster than manufacturers expect, and causes evaporator coils to ice over when airflow drops.

The desert-edge climate adds another layer. Blowing dust from the oil fields and surrounding scrubland loads condenser coils with a gritty film that acts like insulation, raising head pressure and cutting efficiency. A unit that tested at 16 SEER when it was installed may be performing closer to 10 SEER after a few seasons without a coil cleaning — meaning higher utility bills and a compressor that's quietly working toward an early failure.

## Our AC Repair Process in Taft

Every repair call starts with a systematic diagnosis rather than a parts swap. The technician connects manifold gauges to check refrigerant pressures against the manufacturer's charge specifications, measures supply and return air temperatures at the registers, and inspects the condenser coil, blower motor, capacitor, and electrical disconnect. That data points to the actual failure — not just the symptom.

If the diagnosis turns up a refrigerant leak, the leak is located and repaired before any refrigerant is added. Topping off a leaking system is a short-term fix that leaves the underlying problem in place and releases refrigerant into the atmosphere unnecessarily. Once the repair is confirmed, the system is pressure-tested, evacuated, and recharged to the nameplate specification. If a capacitor or contactor has failed — two of the most common causes of a compressor that hums but won't start on a hot afternoon — the part is replaced and the electrical draw is verified before the job is closed out.
