## Why Taft Properties See Repiping Issues

Galvanized steel pipe has a practical lifespan of roughly 40 to 70 years, and most of the housing stock in the 93268 ZIP code is well past that window. The problem isn't just age — it's chemistry. Galvanized pipe corrodes from the inside, building up iron oxide scale that narrows the interior diameter year by year. Homeowners often notice it first as weak flow at the showerhead or a reddish tinge in the water after a vacation. Left alone, the scale eventually creates pinhole leaks or outright bursts.

Taft's desert-edge climate adds a layer of stress that accelerates the timeline. The extreme heat swings between summer afternoons and cool nights cause supply lines to expand and contract repeatedly, working at threaded joints and soldered connections that were already marginal. Homes near the Rails to Trails path corridor and around Taft Heights — many of them built in the 1930s and 1940s — still carry original plumbing that has been patched rather than replaced, which means the next failure is rarely the last one.

Polybutylene pipe is a secondary concern in homes built or remodeled between the late 1970s and mid-1990s. That gray plastic pipe was once marketed as a modern alternative to copper, but it reacts poorly to chlorine in municipal water and becomes brittle over time. If your home has gray plastic supply lines, a repipe is worth prioritizing before a fitting fails unexpectedly.

## Our Whole-House Repiping Process in Taft

A repipe in an older Taft cottage moves differently than work in newer construction. Walls are often plaster over wood lath rather than drywall, which requires more careful access cuts and a different patching approach. We start with a full walk-through to map the existing supply layout, identify where lines run through exterior walls exposed to direct sun, and flag any asbestos-containing materials that may be present in pre-1950 homes — those require a separate abatement step before we open walls.

Once the scope is confirmed and a written quote is approved, the repipe itself typically runs one to two days for a single-family home, depending on square footage and the number of fixtures. We work in copper or PEX-A depending on your preference and budget. Copper is the traditional choice and carries a long track record; PEX-A is flexible, handles freeze-thaw cycling well, and requires fewer fittings inside walls, which reduces future leak points. Both are code-compliant options under California plumbing standards.

All repipe work in Taft requires a permit through Kern County Building and Safety. We pull the permit, schedule the inspection, and close out the paperwork — the homeowner doesn't need to manage that process.
