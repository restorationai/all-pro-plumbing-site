## Common Disposal Symptoms and What They Usually Mean

Most calls we get from Rosedale homeowners fall into a handful of recognizable patterns:

- **Humming but not spinning**: The motor is receiving power but the grinding plate is jammed — often a small bone fragment, a fruit pit, or hardened scale. A reset and manual hex-key turn resolves this maybe half the time; if the plate won't free, the motor windings may be burned.
- **Leaking from the bottom**: The internal seal between the motor housing and the grinding chamber has failed. This is almost always a replacement call, not a repair — the part cost approaches the price of a new mid-range unit.
- **Leaking from the sink flange**: The putty seal at the mounting ring has dried and cracked, which is common in kitchens with significant temperature swings. This is a straightforward reseal or remount.
- **Slow drain with grinding noise**: The disposal itself is functioning, but the drain line downstream is partially blocked. We scope the line before recommending a disposal swap — there's no point installing new equipment into a restricted drain.
- **No power, no hum**: Check the reset button on the unit's base first. If it won't hold, the thermal overload has failed and the unit needs replacement.
