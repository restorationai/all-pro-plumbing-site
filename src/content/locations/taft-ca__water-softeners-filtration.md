## Why Taft Properties Deal with Harder Water Than Most

Taft sits at the western edge of the San Joaquin Valley where the geology is dominated by the same formations that made this region one of California's most productive oil patches. The water supply here picks up dissolved calcium, magnesium, and in some areas trace minerals associated with that subsurface environment. Hardness levels in this part of Kern County routinely test above 20 grains per gallon — well into the "very hard" range on any standard scale.

The housing stock compounds the problem. Ford City and South Taft are filled with workers' cottages built between the 1920s and 1950s, many still running on original or partially replaced galvanized steel pipe. Galvanized pipe that's already corroded on the interior traps mineral scale faster than copper or PEX, narrowing flow and concentrating the taste and odor problems that hard water causes. Installing a softener on a home with galvanized supply lines requires a conversation about pipe condition first — scale has sometimes been the only thing holding a corroded section together, and a sudden change in water chemistry can expose that.

## Our Water Softener and Filtration Process in Taft

Every installation starts with a water test, not a sales pitch. We pull a sample and measure hardness, pH, total dissolved solids, and any secondary concerns like iron or hydrogen sulfide odor — the last one occasionally shows up in older wells and some municipal-connected homes on the west side of town. The test results drive the equipment recommendation, not a one-size-fits-all package.

For most Taft homes, a grain-capacity salt-based ion-exchange softener handles the hardness, and a separate carbon whole-house filter addresses taste and odor. For households that want drinking-quality water at the kitchen tap, we add a reverse osmosis system under the sink — a four- or five-stage RO unit that strips out what the softener doesn't target, including nitrates and residual sediment. We size the RO membrane to actual household demand so the storage tank doesn't run dry during morning routines.

Installation includes setting the bypass valve, connecting to the main supply line, programming the regeneration cycle to run during low-use hours, and testing pressure and flow at completion. We pull any required permits through the City of Taft and schedule the inspection — that step matters for homeowners who plan to sell, because an unpermitted water treatment install can flag during escrow.

## Equipment and Methods for Taft's Water Chemistry

For ion-exchange softeners, we work with units rated for the grain-per-gallon load common in this area — undersizing a softener in Taft means it regenerates constantly, burns through salt, and still delivers partially hard water. Whole-house carbon filtration uses a backwashing media tank rather than a cartridge-style filter, which holds up better when sediment loads are higher and reduces the maintenance burden on the homeowner. Reverse osmosis systems we install include a permeate pump or pressure-boost pump when incoming water pressure is below 60 PSI — older homes near Downtown Taft sometimes have pressure that drops at the meter, and low pressure kills RO production rate.

All equipment we install carries manufacturer warranties, and we document the serial numbers and installation date so warranty service is straightforward if it's ever needed.
