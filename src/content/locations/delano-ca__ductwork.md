## Our Ductwork Repair and Installation Process in Delano

Every job starts with a duct leakage test — we pressurize the system and measure how much air is escaping before we touch anything. That baseline number tells us whether you need targeted duct sealing at a handful of failed joints, a partial duct replacement in a problem zone, or a full ductwork installation if the existing layout is fundamentally undersized or degraded beyond repair. We don't recommend a full replacement when sealing will solve the problem, and we don't patch ductwork that's structurally failing.

For duct sealing, we use aeroseal or brush-applied mastic at joints and connections — not foil tape, which degrades in the heat cycles Delano homes experience every summer. For replacement or new installation, we size duct runs to Manual D calculations so that every room in the house gets the airflow it's designed to receive. We pull the required Kern County mechanical permits and schedule the inspection, so the work is documented and your equipment warranty isn't voided by unpermitted modifications.
