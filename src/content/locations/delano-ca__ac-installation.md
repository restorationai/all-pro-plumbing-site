## Why Delano's Climate and Housing Stock Make AC Replacement a Different Job

Delano sits in the southern San Joaquin Valley, where summer afternoons routinely push past 105°F and the surrounding agricultural fields add humidity during irrigation cycles. That combination puts condensers under sustained stress that accelerates compressor wear, corrodes electrical contacts, and pushes refrigerant pressures to the edge of operating limits season after season. A system that might last 18 years in a coastal climate may be functionally spent in 12 here.

The housing mix matters too. Homes built along the Randolph Street corridor and near Cesar Chavez Park are often pre-1960 construction — smaller duct chases, sometimes no existing ductwork at all, and electrical panels that weren't designed to carry a modern 4-ton split system. Newer subdivisions west of Highway 99 are better suited mechanically, but they tend to have larger square footage and open floor plans that require careful Manual J load calculations to avoid oversizing, which causes short-cycling and humidity problems even when the thermostat reads the right number.

## Local Note: Clay Soil and Condenser Pad Settlement

One thing that catches homeowners off guard in Delano — and across much of the southern Valley — is condenser pad settlement. The clay-dominant soil expands when irrigated and contracts in the dry heat, and over several seasons that movement can tilt a condenser pad enough to stress refrigerant lines and affect compressor oil return. When we replace a unit, we assess the existing pad and pour a new reinforced concrete pad when needed rather than setting a new condenser on a compromised base. It's a small detail that prevents a premature service call two summers from now.

If you're also dealing with scale buildup on your existing equipment — Delano's groundwater is notably hard — that's worth a conversation when we're on-site. Hard water accelerates corrosion on evaporator coil drain pans and affects water heater longevity, and a whole-house filtration or softening solution can protect your new HVAC investment alongside your plumbing.
