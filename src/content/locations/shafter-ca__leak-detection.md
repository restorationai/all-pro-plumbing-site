## Our Leak Detection Process in Shafter

We use a layered approach calibrated to the type of property and the suspected leak location:

**Acoustic listening equipment** picks up the distinct frequency signature of pressurized water escaping a pipe — even through 4 inches of concrete slab or compacted soil. On quiet residential streets near Mannel Park, where ambient noise is low, acoustic detection alone often pinpoints a leak within a foot or two.

**Thermal imaging** reveals temperature differentials on slab surfaces and interior walls. A slab leak running warm water shows up as a heat bloom on a thermal camera long before the surface feels warm underfoot. Cold-water line leaks show the opposite pattern — a cool shadow spreading through the concrete.

**Tracer gas (nitrogen/hydrogen blend)** is introduced into the isolated pipe segment when acoustic and thermal methods narrow the location but can't confirm it precisely. The non-toxic gas migrates through soil and concrete to the surface, where a calibrated sensor detects it. This is particularly useful for deep underground supply lines and the longer lateral runs common in commercial properties near the industrial corridor.

**Pressure testing and isolation** lets us confirm which branch of a plumbing system is losing pressure, ruling out fixtures and isolating the problem to a specific line before any excavation or slab work begins.
