## Our Drain Cleaning Process in Shafter

Every job starts with a conversation about symptoms: which fixture is slow, whether multiple drains are backing up at once (a sign the blockage is in the main line rather than a branch), and how long the problem has been building. That information tells us where to start before we pull a single tool.

For most residential clogs — a shower drain packed with hair and soap scum, or a kitchen sink line coated in grease — a professional cable machine clears the obstruction and restores flow quickly. When scale buildup, root intrusion, or grease accumulation has narrowed a line over months or years, hydro jetting is the right tool. A high-pressure water stream scours the pipe wall clean rather than just punching a hole through the clog, which means the drain stays clear significantly longer.

For lines where we suspect structural issues — offset joints, root damage, or collapsed sections — a camera inspection before or after clearing shows exactly what's happening inside the pipe. That matters especially in the older downtown properties where the original drain layout may not match any surviving blueprint.

## Equipment and Methods We Use for Drain Cleaning

Cable machines (often called rooter service) handle the majority of residential branch-line clogs efficiently. Hydro jetting — typically operating between 1,500 and 4,000 PSI depending on pipe diameter and material — is the preferred method when scale or grease is the primary culprit, which is common given Shafter's hard water. We do not use hydro jetting on pipes that camera inspection shows to be structurally compromised; high pressure on a cracked or offset joint can worsen the damage.

For commercial accounts at the Wonderful Industrial Park or along the Highway 99 corridor, we carry larger-diameter jetting equipment capable of handling floor drains and four-inch commercial mains. Scheduling around operational hours is standard practice — we coordinate access times so a drain cleaning job doesn't shut down a loading dock or a warehouse break room during peak shift.
