## Why Taft's Housing Stock Makes Water Heater Replacement More Involved

Taft sits on the dry, alkaline edge of the San Joaquin Valley, and the water chemistry here is hard on equipment. Mineral scale builds up inside tank heaters faster than it does in coastal climates, shortening the effective lifespan of a standard unit and quietly reducing recovery speed long before the heater actually fails. Homeowners sometimes notice the first sign as a rumbling or popping sound during the heating cycle — that's sediment on the bottom of the tank being disturbed — or as lukewarm showers that get shorter and shorter over a few months.

In the older sections of Downtown Taft and South Taft, the original galvanized steel supply lines compound the issue. Galvanized pipe corrodes from the inside out, and by the time it's 60 or 70 years old, the interior bore can be reduced to a fraction of its original diameter. Installing a new 50-gallon water heater on a half-choked supply line means the new unit never performs the way it should. Our technicians inspect the inlet and outlet connections during every replacement and flag any pipe work that needs to happen alongside the install — so you're not calling us back in six months for a separate problem.

## Our Water Heater Installation and Replacement Process in Taft

Every job starts with a look at what you have: tank size, fuel type (natural gas is the norm in Taft, though electric units appear in some of the older rentals near Taft College), venting configuration, and the condition of the surrounding supply lines. ## Tankless vs. Tank: What Makes Sense in a Taft Home

The case for tankless is real: on-demand heating means no standby heat loss, and a quality unit can last 20 years or more compared to the 10–12 typical of a tank heater in Taft's hard-water conditions. The trade-off is upfront cost and the gas line work that usually comes with it. For a household of two or three people in a smaller Ford City cottage, a well-maintained 40-gallon tank unit with a new anode rod on a regular service schedule is often the practical choice. For a larger family or a rental property where you want fewer service calls, tankless starts to make more financial sense over a 10-year horizon.

We don't push one option over the other. After looking at your home's layout, your gas meter capacity, and your hot water usage patterns, we'll tell you honestly what each path costs and what you get for it.
