## Why Taft Properties See Drain Problems

The same desert-edge climate that keeps Taft dry and dusty year-round concentrates minerals in the local water supply. Hard water leaves calcium and magnesium deposits inside pipes the same way it coats a showerhead — slowly, invisibly, until the opening is narrow enough that grease or soap scum catches and builds a clog. In homes built before 1960, galvanized steel drains compound the problem: the interior surface corrodes and roughens over time, giving debris more texture to grip. That combination of hard water scale and corroded pipe walls is why a drain that seemed fine last season can suddenly back up with almost no warning.

Tree roots are a quieter culprit. Taft's older residential blocks — particularly in the streets radiating out from Downtown Taft toward Ford City — have mature trees whose roots seek moisture in dry soil by finding the nearest sewer lateral. Hairline cracks in aging clay or cast-iron sewer lines are an open invitation. ## Our Drain Cleaning Process in Taft

Every job starts with a drain assessment before any equipment goes into the line. For straightforward kitchen sink clogs — the accumulated grease and food debris common in homes where the original drain slope is minimal — a power auger typically clears the blockage and lets us confirm flow. For recurring clogs, slow drains that have resisted previous service, or any situation where the line is shared between units, we use a sewer camera to locate the exact obstruction and assess pipe condition.

Hydro jetting is the method we reach for when scale buildup or root intrusion is confirmed. A hydro jet delivers water at high pressure through a specialized nozzle that cuts through grease, flushes mineral deposits, and shaves roots back to the pipe wall — leaving a cleaner interior than a cable alone can achieve. For the galvanized and older cast-iron lines common in Taft's pre-war housing stock, we calibrate pressure carefully; pipes that have thinned with corrosion need a measured approach rather than maximum force.
