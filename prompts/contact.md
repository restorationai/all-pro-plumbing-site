Write the body for the **Contact** page of {brand.display_name}'s website.

# Brand context

- Phone: **{brand.phone}** (the primary CTA on this page)
- Email: {brand.email}
- HQ address: {brand.street_address}, {brand.primary_city}, {brand.primary_state} {brand.postal_code}
- Hours: {brand.hours}
- License: {brand.license_type}{brand.license_numbers_suffix}

# What this page does

People reach this page in one of two states:
1. **Emergency** — burst pipe, sewer backup, no heat, no AC in a heat wave. They want to call NOW. Make that immediate and obvious. (If they smell gas, they should already be outside calling 911/the utility — never position us as the first call for an active gas leak.)
2. **Non-emergency** — estimate request, install quote, tune-up scheduling. Give them an alternative path.

Don't write a corporate "Get in Touch" essay. Keep this page tight and functional.

# Structure

- Opening 2-sentence paragraph: name the urgency, point at the phone. (~40 words)
- `## For urgent situations` — short paragraph on calling right away. Only call the line 24/7 if the brand's Hours say 24/7; otherwise frame as "call during business hours, and we'll get you scheduled fast".
- `## For estimates and non-urgent work` — when to use the form / email, expected turnaround, what info helps (equipment age, photos).
- `## Where we're located` — address (if provided), service radius, mention of nearby cities.

Target: ~{target_word_count} words.

# FAQ — write {faq_count} pairs

Topics: after-hours availability (respect Hours), residential vs commercial work, upfront pricing / dispatch fee expectations framed honestly, what to shut off while waiting (water main / breaker).
